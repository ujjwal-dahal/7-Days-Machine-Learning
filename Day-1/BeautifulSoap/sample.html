Most Intense Diseases and Their Mortality Rates

This table provides information about some of the deadliest diseases, their killing rates, and the number of deaths in recent years. The causes of these diseases vary, but they often stem from poor lifestyle choices, environmental factors, and infections.

Causes of the Most Intense Diseases

The diseases listed in the table are influenced by a variety of causes:

  • COVID-19: Caused by the SARS-CoV-2 virus, primarily transmitted through respiratory droplets.
  • Malaria: Caused by Plasmodium parasites transmitted through the bites of infected mosquitoes.
  • Heart Disease: Caused by factors like poor diet, lack of exercise, smoking, and genetic predisposition.
  • Cancer: Caused by genetic mutations, environmental toxins, lifestyle factors like smoking, and certain infections.
  • Diabetes: Caused by insulin resistance or insufficient insulin production, often linked to obesity and poor diet.
